Read this first — how every set works

This is the engine of the whole plan. Understand these six ideas and you can run the program for months without thinking. Every exercise below then gives you the exact set-by-set breakdown.

1 · RIR (Reps In Reserve)

RIR 2 = stop when you could still do 2 more clean reps. It's how hard each set is. Compounds: RIR 1–3. Isolations: RIR 0–1 (closer to failure is safe on machines).

2 · Warm-up sets ≠ working sets

Before the first heavy lift of a muscle, do 2–3 ramp-up sets (light → medium) to prime the joint. These are NOT counted in the "4 sets" — those are your hard working sets.

3 · Double progression

Stay at the same weight until you hit the top of the rep range on every working set. Then add the smallest jump and drop back to the bottom of the range. Repeat forever.

4 · Tempo

Lower under control (~2 seconds), no bouncing, then drive up with intent. The lowering phase is where most growth happens — never let the weight drop.

5 · Rest

Big compounds: 2–3 min (full recovery = more reps = more growth). Isolation / machines: 60–90 s. Don't rush the heavy lifts to save time.

6 · Deload (Week 6)

One easy week built in: drop 1 set per exercise, use ~70% of your normal weight, keep everything at RIR 3+. You'll come back stronger — don't skip it.

⭐ Your picks (incline bench, goblet squat, assisted pull-up) are tagged and placed early in the session while you're freshest.
